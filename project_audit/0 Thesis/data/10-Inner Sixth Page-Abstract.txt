ABSTRACT
Here are the abstract guidelines for a three-paragraph abstract:
Paragraph 1: Background behind the need of selection of this topic
• Introduce the recent scenario
• Clearly state the research question or problem
• Provide a brief overview of the research & significance and relevance
Paragraph 2: Introduction of project work with Methodology and Main Findings
• Describe the research design, methods and procedures used
• Summarize the main findings including any key results or trends
• Highlight any innovative or unique aspects of the research
Paragraph 3: Conclusion and Implications
• Summarize the main conclusions and implications of the research
• Discuss the contributions of the research to the field or industry
• Suggest avenues for future research or potential applications
Note: Keep each paragraph concise and focused, with a total abstract word count of around 250-
300 words.
Keywords in abstract:
• Typically, 5-10 keywords are sufficient for an abstract.
• Use a mix of:2-3 broad keywords (e.g., "vehicle safety", "road accidents")2-3 specific keywords (e.g., "breathalyzer sensor", "real-time alcohol detection")1-2 keywords related to the methodology or approach (e.g., "sensor integration", "software development")
• Avoid repeating keywords excessively; aim for a balanced distribution.
• Use keywords that are directly relevant to the content and focus of the abstract.
